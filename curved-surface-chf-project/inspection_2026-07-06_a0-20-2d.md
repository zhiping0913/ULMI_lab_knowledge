## Symbol meanings

- `K` / `Kappa`: target curvature in units `1/λ0`; `target_curvature = Kappa/laser_lambda0`. For nonzero `Kappa`, `R=1/Kappa`, `target_radius=R*λ0`, `target_f=sqrt((x-target_radius)^2+y^2)-abs(target_radius)`.
- `D`: target thickness in units of `λ0`; `target_thickness = D*λ0`.
- `L`: density-gradient scale in units of `λ0`; `target_Ne_gradient=L*λ0`. If `L>0`, electron density has an exponential ramp in `target_f`; if `L=0`, slab density around `target_f=0`.
- `N`: electron density in units of critical density (`target_Ne=N*laser_Nc`), usually `N=350`.
- Global laser parameters: `a0=20`, `λ0=0.8 μm`, `laser_W0=12.5λ0`, `laser_FWHM=8 fs`.
- Current `start_2D.py` uses file-based initial fields from `../Initialize_Field/` for `Ex`, `Ey`, and `Bz`; the analytic `laser` block is commented out.
- The 45° incidence is encoded in the prepared field/geometry workflow, not as an active `theta` constant in the final deck.
- Caution: directory label `D=...` is not always exactly the deck's physical `D`; for physical thickness, read `input.deck`.
